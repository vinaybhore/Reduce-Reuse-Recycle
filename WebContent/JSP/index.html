World Paper Consumption in 2013

Global Consumption of paper is on the rise reaching 350 million tonnes in 2007 and 370 million tonnes in 2009. If we forecast this value the expected consumption of paper in 2013 is expected to be 413 million tonnes

Total Printing and Writing Sector Consumption

In the printing and writing sector, commercial printing applications consumed the most paper by volume, followed by office copy reprographic paper and paper for mailers and inserts..

Annual Landfill

Paper is the most commonly recycled product, and yet is still one of the largest single components of landfills in the United States, comprising over 16% of landfill deposits equaling 26 million tons annually

Environment

The pulp and paper industry is the fourth largest emitter of greenhouse gases among manufacturing industries

  • Paper recovery for recycling has exceeded 60 percent each year since 2009.
  • The amount of paper and paperboard recovered in the U.S. increased 76 percent between 1990 and 2012.
  • Every ton of paper recycled saves more than 3.3 cubic yards of landfill space.
  • 87 percent (268 million) of Americans have access to community curbside or drop-off paper recycling programs.
  • Total paper recovery in the U.S. exceeded 51 million tons in 2012.
  • By weight, more paper is recovered for recycling from municipal solid waste streams than glass, plastic, steel and aluminum combined.
  • Nearly three times more paper is recycled than is sent to landfills.
  • AF and PA member companies' use of recovered fiber results in avoided greenhouse gas emissions of more than 18.5 million metric tons of CO2 equivalents each year.
  • Approximately 76 percent of all U.S. papermakers use some recovered paper to make everything from paper based packaging to tissue products to office paper and newspaper.
